## Research Profile

My research focuses on quantitative fisheries science, particularly the ecology and stock assessment of marine fishes in the East China Sea, Yellow Sea, Sea of Japan, and western North Pacific. Recent work combines long-term fishery data, CPUE standardization, spatial models, stable isotope analysis, fatty acid composition, and fish biological traits to evaluate changes in fish distribution, population structure, and ecosystem state.

I also develop reproducible simulation and assessment workflows in R, TMB, and Stock Synthesis for evaluating observation error, process error, movement/connectivity, and management procedures in fishery resource assessment.

## Software and Reproducible Research Projects

### GMCS: Growth-mediated Connectivity Simulator

Simulation framework for evaluating whether exchange or movement rates between areas can be inferred from growth-mediated differences in length-at-age distributions. The project is designed for testing connectivity scenarios, observation error, age error, estimator performance, and reproducible simulation workflows.

### TMBSS: TMB-based Short-lived Species Stock Assessment

Prototype stock assessment framework using Template Model Builder (TMB) for short-lived species. The project explores separation of process and observation error, integration over latent states, quarterly dynamics, growth, selectivity, and comparison with Stock Synthesis-style workflows.

### Stock Synthesis / Operating Model Workflows

Simulation workflows for generating known-truth operating model data, fitting Stock Synthesis models, and comparing estimated biomass, spawning biomass, recruitment, CPUE indices, and length compositions with true trajectories.
